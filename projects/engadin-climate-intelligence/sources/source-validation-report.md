## 4. Contradictions Between Sources / Widersprüche zwischen Quellen

### Contradiction 1 (MAJOR / WESENTLICH): Temperature Baseline Inconsistency

**DEE-3 (Research Findings):** "Switzerland's mean temperature is already +3.0°C above the pre-industrial baseline (1871–1900) as of 2026" — **CONFIRMED** by MeteoSwiss.

**DEE-4 (Strategy Findings):** "Swiss mean temperatures already running approximately 2°C above pre-industrial levels (roughly twice the global average)"

**Explanation:** Both figures are technically traceable to MeteoSwiss/NCCS data but describe different things:
- +3.0°C: Switzerland's current (2026) mean temperature vs. 1871–1900 baseline — current, correct for today
- +2.0°C: Switzerland's warming **up to the 1991–2020 reference period** vs. pre-industrial — this was accurate for historical comparison but is not the current temperature anomaly

**Required action / Erforderliche Massnahme:** DEE-4 must be corrected. The strategy document should read: "Swiss mean temperatures are currently approximately **3.0°C** above the pre-industrial baseline (1871–1900), roughly twice the global average of ~1.5°C." Using the IPCC 1850–1900 baseline would give ~+2.7–2.9°C, which should also be cited appropriately.

---

### Contradiction 2 (MINOR / GERINGFÜGIG): Permafrost Warming Rate

**DEE-3:** +0.02°C/year at depth (20m, ice-poor sites, Muot da Barba Peider and Ritigraben) — **Confirmed** by Frontiers paper.

**DEE-4:** +0.5°C/decade (=0.05°C/year) near-surface at Murtèl-Corvatsch since the 1980s.

**Explanation:** These are NOT contradictory if clearly specified: near-surface layers warm faster than deep layers; ice-rich sites (like Murtèl-Corvatsch) can show faster rates than ice-poor sites. However, as written in DEE-4 without depth specification, a reader may conflate the two rates. The difference (0.02 vs 0.05°C/year) may appear contradictory without context.

**Required action / Erforderliche Massnahme:** DEE-4 should specify "near-surface (< 5m depth)" when citing the Corvatsch rate, and note that deep-layer rates are lower (+0.02°C/year at 20m depth as confirmed at Muot da Barba Peider). Both figures are valid for their respective conditions.

---

### Contradiction 3 (METHODOLOGICAL / METHODISCH): COVID-19 Tourism Growth Distortion

**DEE-4:** "Alpine summer tourism bookings in Switzerland grew 18–24% from 2018 to 2024 (Swiss Tourism Federation data)"

**Issue:** The 2018–2024 period includes the COVID-19 collapse of 2020–2022 and subsequent rebound. Measuring growth from a pre-COVID baseline to a post-recovery year inflates the growth figure and may reflect statistical normalization rather than structural expansion.

**Required action / Erforderliche Massnahme:** DEE-4 should either (a) use a CAGR excluding the COVID years (2018–2019 vs. 2023–2024 comparison), or (b) explicitly acknowledge the COVID distortion and note that the 18–24% figure includes pandemic recovery, making the structural growth component smaller. The CHF 80–150M opportunity estimate may need revision if the baseline growth assumption is adjusted.
