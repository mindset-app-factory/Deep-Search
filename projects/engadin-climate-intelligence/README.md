## English

### Overview

This project contains a comprehensive, bilingual climate intelligence report for the Engadin region (Upper Engadin, Lower Engadin, Val Müstair), covering climate change, water resources, snow reliability, and alpine risk scenarios for the period 2026–2036.

**This is a strategic climate and risk intelligence report — not a weather forecast.**

The report is based on scientific sources (MeteoSwiss, IPCC, Swiss Climate Scenarios CH2018), governmental data, and risk scenario frameworks. All major claims are traceable to verified sources.

### Key Findings

- **Temperature**: Switzerland is already +3.0°C above pre-industrial baseline. Alpine regions face +0.6–1.9°C additional warming by 2035.
- **Snow Cover**: Duration shortened by ~8.9 days/decade; reliable natural snow cover retreating above 2,500 m.
- **Glaciers**: Morteratsch, Palü, and Pers glaciers losing 3–6% volume annually. Pers: 4.8 km² → 2.5 km² (−48% since 1990).
- **Water**: Summer river discharge declining up to 40%; extreme precipitation events increasing.
- **Risk**: Infrastructure, tourism, and investment risks elevated across all scenarios.

- **Temperatur**: Die Schweiz liegt bereits +3,0°C über dem vorindustriellen Ausgangswert. Alpine Regionen erwarten bis 2035 weitere +0,6–1,9°C Erwärmung.

- **Gletscher**: Morteratsch, Palü und Pers verlieren jährlich 3–6% ihres Volumens. Pers: 4,8 km² → 2,5 km² (−48% seit 1990).
- **Wasser**: Sommerlicher Flussabfluss sinkt um bis zu 40%; Extremniederschläge nehmen zu.
